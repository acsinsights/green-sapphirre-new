Cookies Policy

Cookies are small text files that websites send to your browsers when you visit them. They are stored on your computer or mobile device and contain information that the website can access when you revisit it later. Cookies serve various purposes, including enhancing your browsing experience and providing personalized content.

Here are some common types of cookies that websites may use:

  • ● Sensor Cookies: These cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. They help websites remember your actions and maintain continuity as you navigate between different pages.
  • ● Persistent Cookies: Unlike Sensor cookies, persistent cookies remain on your device even after you close your browser. They store information such as your preferences and login details, making it more convenient for you to revisit the website in the future.
  • ● First-Party Cookies: These cookies are set by the website you are currently visiting. They are used to improve your experience on that specific site, such as remembering your language preferences or items in a shopping cart.
  • ● Third-Party Cookies: Third-party cookies are set by domains other than the one you are currently visiting. They are often used for advertising and tracking purposes, allowing advertisers to deliver targeted ads based on your browsing behavior across different websites.

It is important to note that while cookies can be helpful for a personalized browsing experience, they can also raise privacy concerns. Many websites provide options for managing or disabling cookies through your browser settings.
